# Clockchain Weekly — May 25

*The one thing that changed our thinking this week:* The product isn't an ID badge for agents. It's a stream of court-proof receipts for everything agents do — and the receipts worth the most are the ones where agents move money.

## What we learned about the product

- An agent gets an ID once, but it acts thousands of times. The value is the ongoing stream of receipts, not the one-time ID.
- The receipts that matter most are payment receipts — proof of who spent what, when, and whether they were allowed to.
- Our real edge is trustworthy time: we can prove exactly when something happened in a way that holds up in court. Nobody else in the agent space can.
- We found a hole in our own plan worth fixing: we'd been leaning on a piece of someone else's standard that turns out to be a payment address, not an identity — so the "which agent did this" link needs its own design.

## What we learned about go-to-market

- The wedge is agent payments. That's where the law already bites — money-laundering and financial rules — and where companies are nervous.
- The unsolved problem: none of those rules were written for an AI agent moving money instead of a person. Any company letting agents spend has no rulebook. That fear is what makes them pay.
- Best first customers: finance, cybersecurity, legal — they already have regulators at the door.
- Bigger opening: industry groups are building a shared standard for agent receipts right now, and every one of them skipped trustworthy time — our exact strength. We could become the time piece everyone plugs into.

## What to watch next week

- Movement on agent-payment rails (x402, Skyfire, Kite).
- Any regulator signaling how AI-agent transactions get treated.
